We start by defining combinatorial surfaces and illustrating the construct with several examples.

Definition: A combinatorial surface is a finite set of \(n\)-gons where every edge identified with a direction and a label, such that every label occurs twice.

Definition: Orientability

Definition: The Euler charateristic of a polygon with \(V\) vertices, \(E\) edges, and \(F\) faces, is given by

\[\chi = V - E +F\]

Theorem: Every connected closed compact surface is homeomorphic to either a sphere, a sphere with \(n\)-handles, or a sphere with \(k\) crosscaps.
